Instructions

Welcome! In this experiment, you'll be reading some short passages and be asked whether these passages make up a coherent story or not. Each passage will be presented to you one word at a time, and after each one, you will be asked to make your judgement.

Each passage will begin with a series of dashed lines, like this:

— —

Once you press the spacebar, the passage will be presented to you one word at a time in the center of the screen. Please read each passage silently to yourself (Do not read out loud!), paying close attention while you are reading so you will be ready to make a judgment about whether the passage was coherent or not.

At the end of each passage, you will be prompted to make your judgment by "???". Please respond quickly and accurately to whether the passage was coherent or not. Press J if the passage is coherent/good, and F if it is not coherent/bad.

You will be asked to take a short break for 10 seconds every now and then between passages, but please do not do so when you are in the middle of a passage.

This experiment requires your full attention. The experiment should take no more than 60 minutes, but during this time you must give your complete attention.

What key do you press to read the passage?

Will the entire passage stay on the screen?

What key do you press to indicate that the passage is NOT coherent?

If you are ready, please turn off any distractions such as music, television, or a cell phone for the next 60 minutes, and click below key to begin the practice session. Thank you!
